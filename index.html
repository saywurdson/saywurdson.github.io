§ 03 Selected Work

Recent projects in healthcare data pipelines and OMOP modeling.

Project 1 data pipeline OMOP v5.4 streamlit

TX Workers' Comp Analytics↗

// 12 raw datasets → 20+ OMOP CDM v5.4 tables

End-to-end pipeline pulling Texas DWC medical billing data from data.texas.gov via the Socrata API, enriched with reference data from RxNav and VSAC FHIR, and transformed through a 3-layer dbt architecture (staging → intermediate → marts).

Maps ICD-9/10 → SNOMED, NDC → RxNorm, and HCPCS/CPT → standard concepts via custom dbt macros, with hash-based deterministic person_id across professional (CMS-1500), institutional (UB-04), and pharmacy (DWC-066) bills. Adds workers'-comp-specific OMOP enrichments — injury-date observations to anchor observation_period, employer FEIN observations for cohort segmentation, and no-fault cost math that surfaces contractual write-offs invisible in standard claims models.

Closes with a 7-tab Streamlit dashboard covering injury profiles, opioid prescribing, provider networks, and geographic distribution.

Project 2 synthetic data OMOP + tuva duckdb

Claims DB↗

// 305-patient ca medicare cohort · two parallel data models

Synthetic 305-patient California Medicare cohort generated with Synthea and exported in two parallel formats: Synthea native CSV (clinical layer in SNOMED, LOINC, RxNorm, CVX) and CMS Blue Button RIF (Medicare claims, pipe-delimited, with real ICD-10-CM and HCPCS codes).

Both formats land in DuckDB and feed two dbt projects in lockstep — OHDSI's dbt-synthea producing OMOP CDM v5.4, and a custom loader producing Tuva input tables plus 17 analytic marts (HCC, AHRQ, CCSR, readmissions, financial PMPM).

The goal is to simulate a harmonized dataset that joins clinical and claims data for the same patients. The core engineering is the BFD↔Synthea identity bridge: BFD truncates patient names to 15/14 characters and emits dates as DD-Mon-YYYY, so a natural-key join handles the alignment and a canonical Synthea UUID becomes person_id across both data models — letting clinical events and claims be stitched together without losing referential integrity.
